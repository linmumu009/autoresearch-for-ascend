# v0.3.4 - Higher LR Probe And 12-Step Verification

Date: 2026-07-03

## What Changed

- Added `framework_adapters/mindspeed_llm/candidates/lr_3em5_6step.env`.
- Added `framework_adapters/mindspeed_llm/candidates/lr_2em5_12step.env`.
- Updated English and Chinese README files, framework evaluation notes,
  changelog, and MindSpeed-LLM adapter notes with the new results.

## Why

The v0.3.3 run showed that `LR=2.0e-5` still improved the 6-step search. This
version asks two follow-up questions:

1. Does the 6-step LR gain keep improving at `3.0e-5`?
2. Does the current `2.0e-5` candidate remain useful when the budget increases
   from 6 to 12 steps?

## Results

| Candidate | LR | Steps | Raw HF Val Loss | Raw HF Val PPL | MindSpeed Valid Loss | Last Train Loss |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `lr_3em5_6step.env` | `3.0e-5` | 6 | 13.120849 | 499243.503 | 0.305617 | 0.491179 |
| `lr_2em5_12step.env` | `2.0e-5` | 12 | 13.097093 | 487523.225 | 0.293768 | 0.477166 |

## Decision

Keep `lr_2em5_12step.env` as the best observed candidate overall.

The 6-step over-shoot boundary is still above `3.0e-5`, but the longer
`2.0e-5` run slightly beats the `3.0e-5` 6-step run on the fixed raw HF
validation surface.

## Next Step

Compare `3.0e-5` and `2.0e-5` under the same 12-step budget, or test a cautious
`4.0e-5` 6-step candidate to keep mapping the boundary.
