# v0.3.5 - 12-Step LR Comparison And 4e-5 Probe

Date: 2026-07-03

## What Changed

- Added `framework_adapters/mindspeed_llm/candidates/lr_3em5_12step.env`.
- Added `framework_adapters/mindspeed_llm/candidates/lr_4em5_6step.env`.
- Updated English and Chinese README files, framework evaluation notes,
  changelog, and MindSpeed-LLM adapter notes with the new results.

## Why

The v0.3.4 run showed that `LR=3.0e-5` was the best 6-step candidate, while
`LR=2.0e-5` remained strong on a 12-step budget. This version compares
`3.0e-5` under the same 12-step budget and keeps mapping the 6-step over-shoot
boundary with `4.0e-5`.

## Results

| Candidate | LR | Steps | Raw HF Val Loss | Raw HF Val PPL | MindSpeed Valid Loss | Last Train Loss |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `lr_3em5_12step.env` | `3.0e-5` | 12 | 12.725430 | 336189.379 | 0.307299 | 0.481535 |
| `lr_4em5_6step.env` | `4.0e-5` | 6 | 12.786428 | 357334.816 | 0.315171 | 0.509350 |

## Decision

Keep `lr_3em5_12step.env` as the best observed candidate overall.

The 6-step over-shoot boundary is still above `4.0e-5`, but the strongest
apples-to-apples longer-budget result is currently `3.0e-5` at 12 steps.

## Next Step

Verify `4.0e-5` under the same 12-step budget, or run a cautious `5.0e-5`
6-step candidate to keep mapping the short-budget boundary.
