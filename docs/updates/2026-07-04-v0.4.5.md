# v0.4.5 - Independent Held-out DPO Scoring

## Scope

Created an independently authored 64-row DPO held-out set for the ms-swift
Qwen3.6-27B DPO experiment. This set is not a slice of `ops_dpo_512.jsonl`.

The generated topics cover:

- shared Ascend job safety
- offline dependency policy
- adapter checkpoint evidence
- FSDP2 checkpoint risk
- learning-rate search
- generation smoke interpretation
- experiment documentation
- data leakage checks

## Results

Held-out dataset: `/workspace/llin-rl-dpo/datasets/ops_dpo_heldout_64_v1.jsonl`.

| Model | Rows | Mean Chosen Logprob | Mean Rejected Logprob | Mean Margin | Win Rate |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |

Per-row deltas:

- margin improved rows: `64/64`
- mean margin delta: `+2.0588`
- median margin delta: `+1.8406`
- minimum margin delta: `+0.5142`
- maximum margin delta: `+4.0433`
- chosen logprob improved rows: `4/64`
- rejected logprob lowered rows: `64/64`

## Conclusion

The `2.0e-4` adapter still improves chosen-vs-rejected margins on an
independently authored held-out set, and win_rate reaches `100%`.

However, the held-out improvement mostly comes from lowering rejected
logprobabilities. Chosen logprob drops on most rows. That means the adapter has
a clear rejection/preference signal, but we should not yet claim improved
open-ended generation quality.
