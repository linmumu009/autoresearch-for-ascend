# v0.4.6 - Held-out LR Tradeoff

## Scope

Saved and evaluated additional `1.0e-4` and `1.5e-4` adapters, then compared
them with the previous `2.0e-4` adapter on the independent held-out DPO set.

## Results

Held-out dataset: `/workspace/llin-rl-dpo/datasets/ops_dpo_heldout_64_v1.jsonl`.

| LR / Model | Mean Chosen Logprob | Mean Rejected Logprob | Mean Margin | Win Rate | Chosen Delta | Rejected Delta | Margin Delta |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Base | `-4.4359` | `-5.6941` | `1.2583` | `93.75%` | - | - | - |
| `1.0e-4` | `-4.3502` | `-6.6573` | `2.3070` | `100%` | `+0.0856` | `-0.9631` | `+1.0488` |
| `1.5e-4` | `-4.9520` | `-7.7374` | `2.7853` | `100%` | `-0.5162` | `-2.0432` | `+1.5271` |
| `2.0e-4` | `-5.4774` | `-8.7944` | `3.3171` | `100%` | `-1.0415` | `-3.1003` | `+2.0588` |

## Conclusion

There is no single best LR unless we define the objective:

- `2.0e-4` is best for maximum DPO margin and strongest rejected-response
  suppression.
- `1.0e-4` is best as the conservative/balanced held-out adapter: it reaches
  `100%` win_rate while slightly improving chosen logprob on average.

For the next experiment, the best default should be `1.0e-4` if generation
quality and avoiding over-suppression matter more than raw DPO margin.
